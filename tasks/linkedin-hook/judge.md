# Scoring Rubric: LinkedIn Hook Task

Score this LinkedIn post draft on 4 dimensions (0.0 to 1.0 each). Average all four for the final score.

## 1. Hook Quality (0.0 - 1.0)
- 1.0: First line stops the scroll. Specific, surprising, creates curiosity gap. Under 50 chars.
- 0.7: Grabs attention but could be sharper. Generic opening.
- 0.3: Forgettable. Could be any LinkedIn post.
- 0.0: Starts with "I've been thinking about..." or similar warm-up.

## 2. Voice Authenticity (0.0 - 1.0)
- 1.0: Sounds like a real person typed this on their phone. Varied sentence lengths. No AI patterns. Punchy.
- 0.7: Mostly natural but has 1-2 AI tells (parallel structure, overly smooth transitions).
- 0.3: Clearly AI-generated. Bullet lists, em dashes, perfect grammar throughout.
- 0.0: Corporate speak or obvious template.

## 3. Concrete Value (0.0 - 1.0)
- 1.0: Reader walks away with a specific, actionable insight. Names tools, numbers, or architecture.
- 0.7: Has some value but stays surface-level.
- 0.3: Generic advice that could apply to anything.
- 0.0: No value — just opinion without substance.

## 4. Engagement Design (0.0 - 1.0)
- 1.0: Ends with a question that makes readers want to share THEIR experience. Designed for saves and comments.
- 0.7: Has a CTA but it's generic ("What do you think?").
- 0.3: No real engagement mechanism.
- 0.0: Ends abruptly or with a link.
